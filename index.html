Marielle Franco

Brazil's favelas mourn the death of a champion.

Here's a time life of Marielle Franco's life

  • 1979 - Born in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
  • 1990 - She began to work to contribute to the household income when she was 11 years old.
  • 1998 - Gave birth to the first and only child when she 19 years old. She raised her daughter without the father's help and worked as a pre-school teacher on a minimum wage.
  • 2000 -She began her pre-university studies. Following a friend's death from a stray bullet, Franco started working in human rights activism.
  • 2002 - She entered the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio de Janeiro on a scholarship and continued working and raising her daughter as she worked towards a degree in social sciences.
  • 2007 - Franco worked as a consultant for the state representative Marcelo Freixo (Brazilian leftist politician, broadcaster and professor). She coordinated the state legislature's Committee for the Defense of Human Rights and Citizenship.
  • 2016 - Franco ran for a seat on the Rio de Janeiro city council in the municipal elections. As a black woman and single mother from the favelas, Franco positioned herself as a representative and defender of poor black women and people from the favelas.
  • 2016 - With over 46,500 votes, Franco was one of the 51 representatives elected, receiving the fifth-highest vote total out of more than 1,500 candidates.
  • 2017 - Working with the Rio de Janeiro Lesbian front, Franco presented a bill to create a day of lesbian visibility in Rio de Janeiro, but a vote of 19-17 defeated the bill.
  • 2018 - On March 13th, Franco spoke out on Twitter against the police violence in Rio de Janeiro.
  • 2018 - The next day, Franco attended a round-table discussion titled "Young Black Women Power Structures". Less than two hours after leaving the round-table, she and her driver, Anderson Pedro Gomes, were fatally shot by two men driving another car.
  • 2018 - Marille Franco were murdered in Rio de Janeiro, at the age 38.
